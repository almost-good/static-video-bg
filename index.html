Dolphin mortality rates used to be around 100,000 a year in the 1970s and they dropped to less than 3000 in 1999, declining further to around 1000 per year more recently. While this is a resounding success story, 1000 dolphins a year is still 1000 dolphins too many, and these deaths can be avoided.
